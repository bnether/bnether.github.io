Aerodynamic Fairing Design

Date: February 2023 â€“ November 2023
Client: Don Short / University of Canterbury


Investigated the aerodynamic performance of a land-speed racer motorcycle, and created designs to further improve the vehicle.

  • Created CAD models of the motorcycle frame and existing fairing design with aid from 3D scanning equipment.
  • Performed computational fluid dynamics (CFD) simulations to predict the vehicle performance.
  • Validated the simulations using a scaled wind tunnel model.
  • Designed a modified aerodynamic fairing to improve the overall performance with relatively simple geometrical modifications, and repeated the above analysis process.
  • Designed an entirely new aerodynamic fairing to reach even greater performance gains, taking into account manufacturing processes.


The motorcycle with the modified aerodynamic fairing has since broken the NZ land speed record for the 50cc partial streamliner class (50-APS-G).
